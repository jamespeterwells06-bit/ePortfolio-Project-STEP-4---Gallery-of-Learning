W200 Microcredential Badges
The following eight badges are microcredentials I earned during the Spring 2026 semester in W200: Current Trends in Educational Technology at Indiana University Bloomington. Each badge is aligned to the ISTE Standards for Educators — a globally recognized framework that defines what effective, ethical, and innovative use of technology looks like for teaching professionals. The ISTE Standards guide educators in becoming learners, leaders, and designers who leverage technology to empower students and transform learning. To learn more about the ISTE Standards, visit iste.org/standards.
ISTE Standard 2.1 — Learner

Learner

Badge: The Scratch Badge

The Learner standard asks educators to continually improve their practice by learning from and with others and exploring proven and promising practices that leverage technology. For this badge, I explored Scratch — MIT's block-based coding platform — and created an original project. Through this experience I gained hands-on understanding of how coding and computational thinking can be introduced to elementary students in a creative, approachable way.

ISTE Standard 2.2 — Leader

Leader

Badge: The Innovator Badge

The Leader standard challenges educators to seek out opportunities for leadership to support student empowerment and success. For this badge, I watched the film "Most Likely to Succeed" and reflected on how innovative school models challenge traditional education. This experience shaped my vision of the kind of teacher and classroom leader I want to become — one who prioritizes student agency and real-world learning over rote instruction.

ISTE Standard 2.3 — Citizen

Citizen

Badge: The Google Digital Safety & Security Badge

The Citizen standard focuses on inspiring students to positively contribute and responsibly participate in the digital world. For this badge, I completed Google's Digital Citizenship and Safety course, passing all five modules. I deepened my understanding of online safety, data privacy, and responsible digital behavior — knowledge I will actively model and teach in my future elementary classroom.

ISTE Standard 2.4 — Collaborator

Collaborator

Badge: The Collaborative Timeline Badge

The Collaborator standard emphasizes working with colleagues and students to improve practice and share resources. For this badge, I designed a collaborative timeline activity for students using digital timeline tools, planning how students could work together to sequence events, add media, and build shared knowledge. This project helped me see how collaborative digital tools can deepen historical thinking and teamwork in elementary classrooms.

ISTE Standard 2.5 — Designer

Designer

Badge: The Graphic Design Badge

The Designer standard asks educators to design authentic, learner-driven activities that recognize and accommodate learner variability. For this badge, I used a digital graphic design tool to create a poster or infographic for students, incorporating varied shapes, colors, text, and images. This experience strengthened my ability to design visually engaging, accessible materials that support diverse learners.

ISTE Standard 2.6 — Facilitator

Facilitator

Badge: The SeeSaw Badge

The Facilitator standard focuses on enabling student learning with technology-rich environments. For this badge, I created a SeeSaw class, explored its features, and invited peers to participate. SeeSaw is particularly well-suited for elementary educators — it allows students to document and share their learning while keeping families connected and engaged in the classroom community.

ISTE Standard 2.7 — Analyst

Analyst

Badge: The NotebookLM Badge

The Analyst standard guides educators to understand and use data to drive instruction and support students in achieving their learning goals. For this badge, I explored Google's NotebookLM — an AI-powered notebook that helps users engage deeply with source material. I uploaded course content and used the tool to study and synthesize information, gaining insight into how AI-assisted tools can support both teacher planning and student learning.

Badge 8 — GenAI for Studying & Learning

GenAI for Studying & Learning

Badge: GenAI 101 Course Completion

For Badge 8, I completed a full section of the GenAI 101 Canvas course, exploring how generative AI tools can be used responsibly for studying and learning. This experience helped me think critically about the role of AI in education — its potential to personalize learning and support students, as well as the ethical considerations educators must keep in mind when integrating these tools in K-12 settings.

W200 · Field Experience

STEM Bus Field Experience

W200: Current Trends in Education Technology  ·  Indiana University Bloomington
📸 The STEM to THEM Mobile Lab, Monroe County Community School Corporation — W200 Field Experience

About the Experience

As part of W200: Current Trends in Education Technology, I participated in a field experience with the STEM to THEM Mobile Lab — a fully equipped, traveling science and technology classroom operated by the Monroe County Community School Corporation (MCCSC). The mobile lab brings hands-on STEM learning directly to schools and communities that may not otherwise have access to advanced resources and equipment. During this experience, I had the opportunity to observe how technology is used as an instructional tool outside of a traditional classroom setting, explore the lab's interactive stations, and reflect on how mobile learning environments can expand educational equity and access for K-12 students throughout Indiana.

Summary of Learning & Takeaways

This field experience challenged me to think beyond the four walls of a classroom. Seeing the STEM Mobile Lab in action highlighted how creative, community-supported initiatives can bring high-quality educational technology to students regardless of their school's resources. I observed how the facilitators used inquiry-based strategies to engage students — asking guiding questions rather than lecturing — an approach I hope to carry into my own future classroom. The experience also deepened my understanding of how partnerships between schools, local businesses, and community organizations can directly benefit students. Sponsors like CenterPoint Energy, Duke Energy, Boston Scientific, and Catalent Biologics made this resource possible, showing me that educators are not alone in building meaningful learning opportunities.
